Organic Peroxide Guide

Dibenzoyl Peroxide: a practical introduction for industrial buyers

Dibenzoyl Peroxide, commonly known as BPO, is one of the most widely used organic peroxide initiators in industrial production. For buyers, the key point is not only the name of the chemical, but also the form, concentration, packaging and application environment.

BPO can be supplied in different commercial forms, including granular, powder and paste grades. These forms are designed to make handling, dosing and dispersion more suitable for different production processes. In many purchasing discussions, the correct grade is often more important than simply comparing price by kilogram.

  • Typical product identity: Dibenzoyl Peroxide (BPO), CAS 94-36-0.
  • Common forms include granular 75%, powder 75% / 50%, and paste 50% / 32%.
  • Selection should consider application, storage conditions, packaging and local handling requirements.

Application Note

Dibenzoyl Peroxide Paste: where it fits in production

Dibenzoyl Peroxide Paste is often selected when easier dispersion and controlled handling are important. Compared with dry forms, paste grades can be more convenient in certain resin, composite and material-processing systems where uniform mixing is part of the production target.

For users, the paste percentage, carrier system and compatibility with the downstream formulation should be checked carefully. A good discussion normally includes the resin type, processing temperature, expected curing behavior, packaging preference and storage plan.

  • Common grades include BPO Paste 50% and BPO Paste 32%.
  • Used in selected resin, composite, polymer and manufacturing processes.
  • Buyers should confirm formulation compatibility and practical handling conditions before bulk orders.

TBPB in FRP and SMC: application value and handling notes

tert-Butyl Peroxybenzoate, usually called TBPB, is used in industrial systems where curing performance, processing stability and final material properties need to be balanced. In FRP and SMC-related applications, TBPB is often discussed as part of a complete curing package rather than as a single isolated raw material.

For FRP and sheet molding compound production, users should pay attention to processing temperature, resin system, inhibitor balance, storage conditions and dosing accuracy. Small changes in formulation or processing can influence curing speed, surface performance and production consistency.

  • Typical product identity: tert-Butyl Peroxybenzoate (TBPB), CAS 614-45-9.
  • Common commercial grade: TBPB 98%.
  • In FRP and SMC, confirm process temperature, resin compatibility and safe storage before use.
